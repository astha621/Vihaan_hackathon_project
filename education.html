KRSNA DESIRES OUR GROWTH


"I feel the limbs of my body quivering and my mouth drying up. My whole body is trembling, my hair is standing on end, my bow Gāṇḍīva is slipping from my hand, and my skin is burning. I am now unable to stand here any longer. I am forgetting myself, and my mind is reeling. I see only causes of misfortune, O Kṛṣṇa, killer of the Keśī demon."

Arjuna describes his moment of weakness right before the commencement of the great Kuru war, that is leading him to despair and depression. Krsna however, after patiently hearing Arjuna out, helps him shift perspective and see things in a newer and fresher light — a more holistic outlook.

Happiness and distress are destined for everybody! If you’re happy today, be assured that distress will follow, and if you’re under the weather today, then be assured that the sun will rise tomorrow. This world is a place of duality, and the dualities will come and go. We must learn to tolerate and eventually transcend, like Arjuna did! It is important that we’re taught the philosophy of the Bhagavad Gita. It is most beautiful, revealing and enlightening. It takes away all pain, anxiety and fear! STRENGTH!

THIS TOO SHALL PASS : IMPERMANENCE OF THIS WORLD


"The material body of the indestructible, immeasurable and eternal living entity is sure to come to an end; therefore, fight, O descendant of Bharata."

Krsna wants us to stay in this world for whatever time is allotted to us and fight! Don’t give up! We must do our assigned duty! The body is temporary and so are the pains and pleasures that come along with it. Don’t be too attached to them. Don’t fall for the trap or illusion of comfort. Nothing is permanent in this world. Everything comes and goes like the seasons in a year …

Krsna describes this world as duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam — temporary and filled with miseries, and so to harbour aspirations to enjoy this world and live comfortably forever, is really missing the point! When we falsely think that we shall never die, and that name, fame, money, power, etc. are the only sources of happiness, things can get a WHOLE LOT UNBEARABLE!

WE ARE NOT THE BODY, WE ARE A SPIRIT SOUL


"Never was there a time when I did not exist, nor you, nor all these kings; nor in the future shall any of us cease to be."

"The soul can never be cut to pieces by any weapon, nor burned by fire, nor moistened by water, nor withered by the wind. This individual soul is unbreakable and insoluble, and can be neither burned nor dried. He is everlasting, present everywhere, unchangeable, immovable and eternally the same."


So important is knowing our identity as the soul for our eternal well-being, that the great Kuru minister Vidura instructed Dhritarashtra in the higher principles of the scriptures.

FRAMEWORK FOR ACHIEVING THE PINNACLE OF ALL PINNACLES


So as Krsna desires and other great spiritualists and devotees of the Lord confirm, we must learn about the soul and seek ourselves. It is very important — as knowledge of ourselves as spiritual beings sets the groundwork on which we can continue to develop spiritually, pursuing and reestablishing our lost connection with Krsna Himself, the Supersoul!

"After attaining Me, the great souls, who are yogīs in devotion, never return to this temporary world, which is full of miseries, because they have attained the highest perfection."


This highest perfection is pure bliss, or happiness in all its glory. It is what we constantly and ever-increasingly experience when we meet the Lord face-to-face. Isn’t this what we all seek? To be eternally, infinitely and ever-increasingly happy?

5 / 6

NOT YOUR BODY!


You know suicide is never an option!
This body is not yours! It is given to you for a reason. And everything that comes along with the body must be taken in stride and dealt with accordingly. When Krsna asks us to tolerate pains and pleasures, He wants us to transcend material reality, and be situated in the spirit.
"If we’re not dead, we’re only getting stronger! Believe! That’s what Krsna wants us to do!"


When Arjuna decided to give up fighting and retire to the forest, Krsna reproved Arjuna, and incited him to use his abilities for the Lord’s pleasure and not his own. Arjuna’s body was not his own property, but an instrument of the Lord, who wanted to do great things through Arjuna!

6 / 6

HEART OF A DEVOTEE : REVERSALS AND UPHEAVALS

A devotee of the Lord — a transcendentalist always sees reversals and misfortune in life as the blessings of the Lord. In fact, it is the special mercy of the Lord that all our so-called friends, society, attachments, wealth, etc. wither away. When advanced devotees find themselves at the threshold of great loss and sufferings, they rejoice, for they know it is the special mercy of the Lord. Devotees take reversals as a signal to strengthen their meditation on the Lord, and deepen their relationship with Him. No more worldly distractions!
Devotees of the Lord are fearless! Absolutely fearless beings!
